# Tyled

Fast-paced real-time strategy game played on a tiled board. Each player controls a character and spreads their color by shooting a beam in a straight line. The beam paints every tile it touches in the player’s color, reaching all the way to the edge of the board.

Once a tile is claimed, it becomes permanently locked in that player’s color and cannot be converted by the opponent. Players take damage when stepping on tiles colored by the opponent, making aggressive moves risky.

Each shot consumes a portion of the player’s power bar. When the bar reaches zero, the player can no longer shoot, requiring careful management of offensive resources and timing.

Players can also parry enemy beams. If a tile of the opponent’s color reaches them, they can intercept it, instantly reversing the beam back in their own color. Parrying does not consume any power from the player, allowing for tactical counters and sudden shifts in control.

The board evolves dynamically as players race to claim territory, control key lanes, and outmaneuver their opponent. Victory comes from dominating the board, forcing the enemy into dangerous tiles, and skillfully balancing aggression, defense, and resource management.
